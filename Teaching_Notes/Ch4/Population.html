Population Growth

Teaching Notes from Our World in Data



About these Teaching Notes

These teaching notes are part of a series of resources from Our World in Data.
They have been designed to support those interested in teaching and learning about global development, and they require no background knowledge.

Here we touch on the following questions:
How many people live around the world today?
Will humanity keep growing until collapse?
How will population dynamics change in the next decades?
What’s the link between population growth and development?
What specific aspects of development lead to smaller families?


For more teaching material visit: ourworldindata.org/teaching

This process is called the 'demographic transition'

About Our World in Data:
Our World in Data is an online publication that shows how living conditions are changing. The aim is to give a global overview and to show changes over the very long run, so that we can see where we are coming from, where we are today, and what is possible for the future.
